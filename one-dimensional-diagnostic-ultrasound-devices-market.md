<p><p>One-dimensional diagnostic ultrasound devices are categorized into several types based on their imaging capabilities and applications. </p><p>- A-Type (Amplitude): Primarily used for measuring the amplitude of echoes, it's common in ophthalmic examinations.</p><p>- M-Type (Motion): Designed for real-time motion analysis, often utilized in cardiac assessments to visualize heart movements.</p><p>- D-Type (Doppler): Focuses on blood flow detection, aiding in vascular studies and prenatal monitoring.</p><p>- Other: Includes specialized devices that may integrate features from the above types or cater to niche applications.</p></p>
