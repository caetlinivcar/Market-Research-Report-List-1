<p><p>The photovoltaic glass market features three primary types: polycrystalline, monocrystalline, and thin film. Polycrystalline glass is made from multiple silicon crystals, offering a cost-effective solution with moderate efficiency. Monocrystalline glass, created from single silicon crystals, boasts higher efficiency and a sleek design but at a premium price. Thin film technology utilizes various materials, such as cadmium telluride or amorphous silicon, allowing for lightweight and flexible applications, though it generally provides lower efficiency compared to crystalline options.</p></p>
